United States Wildfire Data

Wildfires don’t stop where the wilderness ends. They burn through communities and neighborhoods, destroying property and taking lives. With climate-driven increases in wildfires in the U.S., it is imperative to understand how the risk to homes is also changing nationwide. The convergence of warmer, drier conditions and greater development into flammable landscapes is leaving many communities vulnerable to human-caused wildfires

The objective of this project was to extract and explore a publicly available database of wildfires that occurred in the United States from 1992 to 2015 and includes 1.88 million geo-referenced wildfire records, representing a total of 140 million acres burned during the 24-year period.

The amount of data was to large to be run on local machines and instead required some sort of filtering to obtain a manageable size dataset that could be handled within a team setting. The data was filtered and selected in a manner that would provide an interesting overview of the fire hazard and damage done in the United States over the pact couple of decades while reviewing a small sample of the total data.
